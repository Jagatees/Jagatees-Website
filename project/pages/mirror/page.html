Man in the mirror

1 Jan 2024

Technology stack

"Man in the Mirror" is an innovative virtual reality game developed for Google Cardboard, aimed at educating users about the effects of drugs. As part of a dynamic three-person team, my role was integral in refining and enhancing the project. My responsibilities included cleaning up the existing code, redesigning scenes, and ensuring the game's successful release on the store.

One of my notable contributions was the development of an interactive fish system within Unity, which added a unique element to the game. Additionally, I was responsible for creating new scenes that were featured in the game's trailer. These enhancements significantly contributed to the game's immersive experience.

Our project was a collaborative effort with the Central Narcotics Bureau (CNB) of Singapore. Their involvement and support were crucial in bringing this educational VR experience to fruition. The partnership with CNB underscored the game's importance and relevance in today's societal context, particularly in addressing drug-related issues.

The journey of working on "Man in the Mirror" was not only a testament to our team's technical abilities but also our commitment to creating meaningful, impactful digital experiences. The game's launch is a significant milestone in our endeavor to use technology for social good.
